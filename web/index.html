The frame is your photo after tearing; the half-dimmed strips print past the tear line (torn off with the stubs), and darker areas never reach paper. The tear/edge lands inside the dashed band.

Printer

—

Borderless trim calibration i The ruler page prints through the exact same pipeline as photos. Hold it so the T reads on top. Each ruler counts mm inward from the image edge, so part of it lands beyond the paper edge or on the tear-off stub. Per edge, read the number where the paper edge or tear line crosses the ruler and enter it below — every print is then shifted & bled by that much so your crop lands edge-to-edge. Sanity: the 50 mm bars must measure 50 mm, and ink should reach a few mm past both tear lines. A white gap before a tear line means the media variant is misconfigured, not something to calibrate around.
